In so called mean time, we agreed the proposal for those processes to be covered in Adobe Sign. Please find notes from today’s call:
Adobe Sign will serve as the interim solution for approval workflows until Collectoid's approval module is fully operational and ready for deployment.
Collectoid Approval module must include, at minimum, a comprehensive audit trail capability before it can replace Adobe Sign. This audit trail functionality is essential to maintain compliance and tracking requirements currently satisfied by Adobe Sign.
Formal signatures from Therapeutic Area (TA) Leads captured through Adobe Sign are considered fully acceptable and compliant, as the platform provides complete audit trail documentation with associated timestamps for all approval actions.
The upcoming signoff for the Biopharmaceutical Agreement of Terms (AoT) will be conducted through Adobe Sign. Marcin to configure the approval form for the Biopharmaceutical TA Lead.
For cross-TA data collection studies, all relevant TA Leads must provide their signatures for the applicable study list within their respective therapeutic areas. The approval process operates on an "all or nothing" principle. If any single TA Lead (e.g., CVRM) fails to provide signature approval, the data collection cannot be enabled for users in any of the therapeutic areas involved. All required signatures must be obtained before provisioning can proceed.
Method 1, as illustrated in the Adobe Sign mockup (URL reference HERE), has been selected as the preferred approval workflow. The Data Privacy Office (DPO) team will extract all necessary information from this method to establish the appropriate policy framework.
The DPO must be notified upon completion of form signoff. Marcin will configure the Adobe Sign form to automatically include the DPO email address (DPO-Data-ProvisioningOperations@astrazeneca.com) as a recipient of the completed form copy. The DPO will also have access to form’s Audit Trail and data recorded in the form itself.
When an approval decision changes - for example, if Study X was initially rejected for provisioning in version 1 but subsequently approved for provisioning, a new version (version 2) must be created with a new TA Lead signature recorded. All decision changes require formal signature documentation to maintain audit trail integrity.
The long-term objective is for the Collectoid to automatically execute approved decisions by provisioning authorised studies to the users specified in the Agreement of Terms, streamlining the end-to-end workflow.
