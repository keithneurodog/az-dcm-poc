# Introduction

## Document purpose and scope

This document defines the procedure for achieving *Role-based Open
Access to Strictly Confidential R&D Clinical Trial Data*, including the
key concepts, process steps and role accountabilities. The document is
designed to be read and understood by data owners and their delegates,
data consumers, plus associated stewards and administrators of the
process.

The scope of this procedure is limited to internal data sharing that
occurs under the accountability of AstraZeneca R&D and limited to use of
R&D's clinical trial data that is classified as strictly confidential.

## Business context

*'Role-based Open Access Model' to Strictly Confidential R&D Clinical
Trial Data* (hereafter referred to as the '*Role-based Open Access
Model' or ROAM*) is an access paradigm designed to increase scale and
speed of access across R&D. The paradigm works through a proactive
top-down approval of 'data collections' and associated 'terms of use' --
whereby groups of data consumers are granted open access to collections
of data. Unlike 'request-based access' (time bound, and tied to
individual project level intent), through ROAM, users are continuously
covered by the top-down agreement (from Data Owners / Delegates) for
qualified use, thus removing the need for repetitive individual
approvals.

## Limitations on the purpose & scope of ROAM

ROAM is designed to enable scale and speed in internal sharing of
scientific data - within the bounds of existing Global Standards,
Standard Operating Procedures, and boarder company data / cyber-security
policies.

ROAM will operate under the following structures for internal sharing of
scientific data, where the new model formalises a new paradigm of open
access within these guardrails -- specific practical details, key
concepts and process steps in the new model will be outlined further
down in this document:

- Global Standard = [Global Standard -- Internal Sharing of Scientific
  Data](https://astrazeneca-eqv.veevavault.com/ui/#doc_info/573078/3/0=&idx=0&pt=asl&sm=122072611763576883132&tvsl=JnNlYXJjaD0lMjU3QiUyNTIydGV4dCUyNTIyJTI1M0ElMjUyMlNUTkQtMDAwMTQ5OCUyNTIyJTI1N0QmaXZwPTEmaXZyPTAmaXY9MSZpdm89ZGVzYyZpdnM9JmZjYWM9JmFsbFN0dWRpZXNTaXRlcz0mc209MTIyMDcyNjExNzYzNTc2ODgzMTMyJnNtYXJ0PXRydWUmaXZ2PUNPTVBBQ1Qmdmlld0lkPWFkdlNlYXJjaCZmYWNldHNVbmNoYW5nZWQ9ZmFsc2U)

- Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) = [Internal Sharing of Scientific
  Data](https://astrazeneca-eqv.veevavault.com/ui/#doc_info/573077/2/0)

- Other = [Management of Important New Safety
  Issues](https://astrazeneca-eqv.veevavault.com/ui/#doc_info/1106560/3/0)

The access model [does not]{.underline} override existing best practices
and requirements. For example:

- The access model does not override the processes required to be
  followed when a safety signal relating to use of an AZ product is
  identified.

- The access model does not override the need to follow the approval to
  publish process.

- The access model does not override the need to handle strictly
  confidential data with care according to cybersecurity best-practices,
  e.g., where data can be stored or what systems can be used for
  processing those data.

All users of ROAM will be required to complete the compulsory training
as set out in the User Guide -- before any internal sharing of
scientific data is actioned.

# Key Concepts

Stated simply, this model is designed to enable access by a defined
scope of data-consumers, to a defined scope of data, under an agreed set
of terms of use. Specific concepts within the model are defined below.

## Open Access Collection (OAC) Agreement

An **Open Access Collection** refers to a specified scope of strictly
confidential clinical trial data that consumers can potentially be
allowed to openly access under ROAM. The formal document within which an
Open Access Collection is agreed and defined is called the **Open Access
Collection (OAC) Agreement** or **OAC Agreement** for short.

An Open Access Collection is defined using multiple criteria that
specify a particular data-scope, and the base data usage boundaries
associated with that scope of data. These criteria can refer to any
aspect of the data, including logical aspects (e.g., the date it was
created), business-based aspects (e.g., what projects generated the data
such as a clinical study), scientific aspects (such as what disease
model), or technical aspects (such as file formats). Further key details
associated to data-scope criteria and definition are:

- **[Inclusion vs Exclusion:]{.underline}** Data scope can utilise
  *inclusions* (ie: data that is included within the Open Access
  Collection), and *exclusions* (ie: data that is omitted from the Open
  Access Collection)

- **[Inclusion Criteria:]{.underline}** For each inclusion criteria
  there is a mechanism to manage how datasets will be included that can
  be one of the following:

  - **Included:** Included by default automatically (accountable parties
    -- Data Owners / Delegates -- retain the right to exclude studies by
    exception)

  - **Included, opt-out:** Included by default, but with an active
    review process to allow for opt-out (approval in Quarterly Approval
    Meetings / offline sessions)

  - **Included, opt-in:** Included only when an active review process
    has chosen to opt-in (approval in Quarterly Approval Meetings /
    offline sessions)

- **[Exclusion Criteria:]{.underline}** **Excluded studies** are always
  removed from scope by default, which can be rule based exclusions, or
  specific to certain studies.

- **[Risk Criteria:]{.underline}** Where a dataset meets multiple
  criteria, the stricter of the two inclusion mechanisms will be
  chosen). New Open Access Collections should be attributed with base
  restrictions and rules will apply to any potential use.

Despite a fixed scope of data, the actual datasets held within such a
collection are not necessarily fixed owing to the dynamic nature of data
lifecycles and the creation, movement, archival and removal of data over
time. This allows Open Access Collections to grow and change, to remain
relevant and maximally useful, while the risk profile of open access can
remain unchanged.

An Open Access Collection may be an existing or new group of data, a
catalogued 'data product', or a virtual set of data with already
established rules. However, in some cases, an Open Access Collection may
need to be [newly defined]{.underline} to support a particular open data
access need. Section 4 below contains further details on the end-to-end
process for setting up & maintaining an Open Access Collection.

When a new Open Access Collection is required, an *Open Access
Collection (OAC) Agreement* should be completed. This document supports
definition of data scope, including opt-in and opt-out requirements;
associated agreement of the business process required to process opt-in
and opt-out datasets over time; and base restrictions on use.

On completion the *Open Access Collection (OAC) Agreement* should be
reviewed and approved by relevant Delegate Data Owner(s) to allow onward
open access to be set up.

## Data Use Terms (AOT)

A **Data Use Terms (AOT)** is a formally written, and approved,
document for open access that is used to define an agreed terms of use
applied to an Open Access Collection. For simplicity, [one]{.underline}
AOT specifies access to just [one]{.underline} Open Access Collection.
However, there can be more than one AOT per Open Access Collection, as
required by different user groups.

There are four main elements that constitute a Data Use Terms
(AOT), these are:

1)  **[Base Definition:]{.underline}** A base definition of the
    Data Use Terms, including elements like an identifier and
    descriptive name, for reference purposes.

2)  **[Data Scope:]{.underline}** A defined scope of data, by
    referencing a predetermined Open Access Collection, or an existing
    data product or data within a system when required.

3)  **[Terms and Conditions of Use:]{.underline}** Terms and conditions
    that define the permitted use of the data. These permitted uses are
    written as a series of rules that refer to aspects such as the
    permitted analysis purposes. Terms and conditions may be inherited
    from the terms already specified for the given data, or be
    introduced in Data Use Terms, as additional or overriding new
    rules

4)  **[User Scope:]{.underline}** A defined scope of data consumers who
    will gain open access to the data. These can be people, or systems
    (including AI systems). They can be explicitly named individuals,
    or, identified by criteria -- such as by management reporting lines
    or role profile types -- so that pools of users may also (like data
    in collections) retain a given scope but change in membership over
    time, e.g., with new joiners and leavers.

# Role Accountabilities

There are three roles that wield accountability in ROAM*.* These are:

1)  **[Data Owner or Delegate Data Owner]{.underline}** -- Data Owners
    (as defined by AstraZeneca R&D's [Data Accountability
    Framework](https://azcollaboration.sharepoint.com/sites/SS647/SitePages/Data-Accountability-Framework.aspx))
    are accountable for data, including how that data is used and any
    risks associated. Data Owners typically operate through assigned
    Delegate Data Owners to actively wield that accountability. Within
    ROAM, the Delegate Data Owners accountability is to approve the
    creation of any new Open Access Collection (in the *OAC Agreement*)
    and approve the *Data Use Terms* associated to their data. Thus,
    they approve who accesses what data and the associated terms and
    conditions of use.

2)  **[Data Consumer Lead]{.underline}** -- Data Consumer Leads are
    accountable for work done by their staff. Data Consumer Leads are
    required to review and abide by the AOT, to ensure that the access
    their teams will gain will provide the desired business benefit,
    whilst committing to the bounds of the agreement. After data access
    is granted, the Data Consumer Lead is accountable for ensuring that
    all associated data use - done by their staff - falls within the
    bounds of the agreement both with reference to (i) what users
    achieve open access and (ii) that all data usage falls within
    permitted terms and conditions of use.

3)  **[R&D Data Office Lead]{.underline}** -- The Data Office Lead owns
    the creation and definition of both the *OAC Agreement* and the
    *AOT*. They facilitate processes for open access setup, subsequent
    maintenance operations and compliance audit. The R&D Data Office
    Lead is required to approve the AOT to both assert that the AOT is
    within the bounds of R&D's broader data access policies, and as
    agreement to commit to initiation of delivery, change management for
    roll-out and maintenance of associated Open Access Collections and
    data access provisioning.
