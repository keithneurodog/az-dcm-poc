## Data exchange ecosystem -- challenges / solutions {#slide-1}

## Problem statement  {#slide-2}

AstraZeneca is experiencing recurring delays in vendor data transfers and partner collaborations primarily due to gaps in contracting and consent reviews. Informed Consent Forms (ICFs) are not consistently reviewed before contracts are signed, creating the risk that AZ ingests data that does not comply with ICF permissions and objectives. Contracts are frequently concluded without essential data protection terms (e.g., data protection clauses and required Standard Contractual Clauses), triggering post‑signature amendments and slowing data flow. Amendment execution is further delayed by dependencies on teams with limited capacity or experience in data privacy and contracting, forcing risk‑based decisions on whether to proceed without the required terms. Delivery teams struggle to find and apply the right templates and lack confidence negotiating privacy and contract language, increasing reliance on scarce specialist support. Collectively, these gaps prolong timelines, heighten legal and regulatory risk, reduce operational efficiency, and erode collaborator experience, undermining DOV's R&D Data Office scorecard goals for compliant, timely data flow.

## Solutions: Get in front of the problem! {#slide-4}

Map and proactively engage teams involved in data-related contracting (procurement, study management, adjacent functions) to identify high-risk groups, understand recurring gaps and provide early, targeted guidance to prevent delays and ensure compliant data flows.

4

Identify at-risk teams:

Establish a pre-approved vendor list by partnering with procurement to maintain vendors with vetted contracts containing privacy appendixes and Standard Contractual Clauses, relevant due diligence enabling teams to proceed with data transfers quickly and confidently without additional legal compliance checks.

Establish pre-approved vendor list:

Review and modernize contract templates to ensure they include the correct data protection appendixes and SCC's, closing known gaps and enabling consistent, compliant contracting.

Review and upgrade contract templates:

Create easy-to-use practical contracting and privacy toolkits that include check-lists, playbooks, and decision trees, and offer targeted training to help teams select and complete the correct templates confidently and consistently.
