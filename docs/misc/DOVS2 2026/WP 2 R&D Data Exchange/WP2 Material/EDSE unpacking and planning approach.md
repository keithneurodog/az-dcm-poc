## Introducing the EDSE challenge {#slide-3}

3

NOTE:

Here we use EDSE (external data sharing ecosystem) as a generic umbrella term for all aspects of external data sharing that we wish to clarify our position, priorities, role and roadmap. We should not get hung up on this EDSE label, rather we should define what we truly mean (scop, objectives priorities, our role  etc ) as part of the proposed exercise.

KEY POINTS ON THE CHALLENGE:

External Data Sharing is a complex landscape with multiple patterns, stakeholders, steps. As use of data grows, so does the need for AZ to share data with external parties (be those vendors, collaborators or otherwise) to generate, share and  analyse  data, for AZ's needs or as part of an external community.  

Data Office has always played a role in these processes (with a 'collaborations' team that has extended and diversified into contracting, plus operational capabilities to identify, move and manage some types of external data). However, changes over time in need, scale,  organisations  and prevalent patterns of external data sharing have occurred. Presently, our overall holistic plan, roadmap and role are unclear. Furthermore, there may be gaps in existing AZ R&D (or wider) EDSE-oriented capabilities that must be filled to meet expected scale, compliance or overall business goals.

In addition to a strategic roadmap, we face acute needs to meet current demand, e.g., contracting.

Hence, we should devise a strategy and roadmap, with clear immediate and longer-term priorities for EDSE-capabilities, to enable the business, and to take forward into an EDE setting.

2)  Identify EDSE Patterns:  Agree the set of different patterns for external sharing, DOs role in each and set a target state.

3)  Prioritise  work on these patterns:  In addition to near term plan, set priority for articulated target states associated to the patterns

4)  Design the solution:  Design the solution required to achieve the target state, and charter & agree project
