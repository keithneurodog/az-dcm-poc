# Document context, purpose and key definitions

A *Data Use Terms (AoT)* is used to define an agreed terms of use
applied to an *Open Access Collection*. The creation of an *Agreement of
Terms* sits as part of the wider process of the *90:10 Open Access
Model* -- this is Step (C) in the end-to-end process below, where
additional information can be found in the '[*90:10 Open Access Model --
Guidance*](https://azcollaboration.sharepoint.com/sites/VS2.Transformingdatasharingaccess2/_layouts/15/guestaccess.aspx?share=Ee-e09KqmzdHrdL-sMXYSR0BdR53r-s2EylcsCNfYgxHFg&e=WdMato)'
document.

![A diagram of a process AI-generated content may be
incorrect.](media/image1.png){width="6.268055555555556in"
height="3.5027777777777778in"}

There are four main elements that constitute a Data Use Terms
(AOT), these are:

1)  **[Base Definition:]{.underline}** A base definition of the
    Data Use Terms, including elements like an identifier and
    descriptive name, for reference purposes.

2)  **[Data Scope:]{.underline}** A defined scope of data, by
    referencing a predetermined Open Access Collection, or an existing
    data product or system when required.

3)  **[Terms and Conditions of Use:]{.underline}** Terms and conditions
    that define the permitted use of the data. These permitted uses are
    written as a series of rules that refer to aspects such as the
    permitted analysis purposes. Terms and conditions may be inherited
    from the terms already specified for the given data, or be
    introduced in Data Use Terms, as additional or overriding new
    rules

4)  **[User Scope:]{.underline}** A defined scope of data consumers who
    will gain open access to the data. These can be people, or systems
    (including AI systems). They can be explicitly named individuals,
    or, identified by criteria -- such as by management reporting lines
    or role profile types -- so that pools of users may also (like data
    in collections) retain a given scope but change in membership over
    time, e.g., with new joiners and leavers.

The outcome of creating the *Data Use Terms* is that R&D Data Office
will pursue Step (D) in the end-to-end process -- initiating Technical
Implementation, User Guides & Training, and Comms & Change Management.

### 2.3.1 Requirements

To use data under this Agreement, the following must be met and
documented to ensure scientific utility, compliance, and privacy per the
Global Standard:

- **Request Review:** The *90:10 Open Access Model* has an established
  procedure to evaluate the risk and benefits of sharing -- this aligns
  with the Due Diligence Process in the Global Standard.

  - **Scientific Merit:** R&D Data Office will document a single
    Research Analysis Plan (RAP) for this Agreement -- capturing the
    approved broad intent, permitted use, user groups, and data scope as
    endorsed by Delegate Data Owners

  - **Contractual Restrictions and Data Ownership:** R&D Data Office
    will perform an assessment of contractual restrictions (divestments,
    out-licensing, in-licensing) before granting access to data in a
    collection

  - **Data Availability:** In cases where AstraZeneca does not have
    access to the data -- it will not be possible to share the data

  - **Legal and Ethical Basis for Sharing:** Permitted use under this
    Agreement is limited to Primary Use only (*more details on Primary
    Use below in 'User Terms of Use'*). Data access must align with the
    study's Protocol/CSP and Informed Consent Form (ICF), and applicable
    privacy laws (eg: GDPR, HIPAA). For China‑origin data, HGR
    requirements must be confirmed and satisfied prior to sharing

  - **Business Sensitivity:** By default, closed studies (\>6 months
    post‑DBL) are eligible for inclusion in the open collection. By
    exception, GPTs or Delegate Data Owners may flag and exclude
    specific studies due to business sensitivity. Exclusions must be
    communicated by Delegate Data Owners to the R&D Data Office.

- **The Principles of Data Protection:** AstraZeneca is required to
  comply with many legal obligations when it uses clinical trial data.
  AstraZeneca has a commitment to apply industry best practices to
  protecting data through (1) Data sub-setting, (2) Secure
  Environment, (3) Legal Protection.

  - **Data Sub-setting:** Since the scope of permitted use under this
    Agreement is limited to Primary Use -- no sub-setting is required,
    full patient populations and associated data are applicable

  - **Secure Environment:** All sharing of Strictly Confidential data
    will take place in an approved, secure environment, that is password
    protected and up to date with industry best practice security
    features, as well as undergoing active security monitoring.

  - **Legal Protection:** As per the Global Standard - AstraZeneca
    employees are expected to abide by the AstraZeneca Code of Ethics
    and Data Privacy Standard, and have the following obligations to
    safeguard patient privacy when using Personal Data:

    a)  Use the data only for the agreed purpose

    b)  Not attempt to establish the identities of individuals

    c)  Not copy any of the content of the database in any form for use
        outside the approved secure environments

    d)  Inform the relevant Safety Representatives of any potential
        product safety issues as soon as they are identified, in line
        with the Management of Important New Safety Issues SOP.

    e)  Ensure that only the minimum amount of Personal Data necessary
        is used to meet the research purpose

    f)  Not download or copy the Personal Data in any form for use
        outside the approved Analysis Environment(s)

    g)  Where the Analysis Team has copied data to a secure Analysis
        Environment, ensure the complete deletion of this copy of the
        data where appropriate.

    h)  Do not disclose Personal Data to any individuals other than
        those listed in this *Agreement*, who have a scientific research
        need to access the data in order to conduct analysis

    i)  Ensure any external parties involved are under confidentiality
        and data privacy obligations to protect the data.

> Data Consumer Leads are accountable for ensuring their teams accessing
> data under this *Agreement* comply with the above statements.

- **Share Insights:** This *Agreement* will continue the emphasis of the
  policy -- to generate new insights from analysis to build scientific
  leadership. For that purpose, analysis and outcomes will be made
  available, so that they are shared and can be built upon in the
  AstraZeneca community. Users through this *Agreement* will continue to
  have the following obligations related to sharing of insights:

  - Ensure the outcome of the research is shared with the Product Team
    within appropriate timeframes

  - Ensure analysis results are vetted with the Product Team and other
    relevant stakeholders prior to use for internal decision making or
    external publications or communications

  - Where results are intended for publication, follow AstraZeneca's
    publication process outlined in the AZ Global Publications Standard.
    Additional review processes, such as consortium or external steering
    committee review, will be undertaken where applicable.

- **GxP Requirements:** To safeguard validity of the data, the users
  under this *Agreement* will conduct or recreate in a GxP environment
  any analyses that are intended for decision making during the product
  lifecycle or submission activities. Users will also store outputs in a
  GxP environment.
