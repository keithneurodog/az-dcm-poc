# Awareness

## Purpose & Scope

The **90:10 Open Access Model** is a new R&D paradigm rolling out from
2025 that gives approved users role‑based, default‑open access to
defined data collections -- reducing case‑by‑case approvals while
maintaining compliance. It is designed to accelerate impact while
managing risk. This guide sets out what's changing, how to access and
use data responsibly, and where to get support -- so you can move from
idea to outcome faster and compliantly.

By the end, you will know:

- What Open Access means for you and how it differs from
  project‑by‑project approvals

- The end‑to‑end journey for using Open Access Collections and the steps
  to follow

- What analysis intent is in scope (Primary Use) vs out of scope
  (Secondary Use)

- Your responsibilities for security, privacy, and publication

- How to plan, access, execute, and close-down a project within Open
  Access, and where to get help

We have structured this User Guide to align to the typical User Journey

## What's New

R&D Data Office is moving from individual, intent‑based approvals to a
role‑based open access model. The target operating principle is 90:10
Open Access Model -- where 90% of user needs are made open by default
for qualified use, while the remaining 10% (complex or edge cases)
continue through individual request-based models. Upfront, top‑down
approvals and proactive data preparation expand the accessible data
estate and reduce time‑to‑access, enabling broader, faster use.

Key changes we are introducing:

- **Role‑Based Access:** Permissions aligned to job role rather than
  project intent, with embedded privacy, security, and auditability.

- **Primary Use reinterpretation:** Data Governance Board decision to
  expand the interpretation of Primary Use (retiring Extended Primary
  Use -- EPU), unlocking more patient data for scientific analysis.

- **90:10 Open Access Model:** End‑to‑end operating process - top‑down
  approval by Data Owners/Delegates; coordinated rollout and stewardship
  by the R&D Data Office.

- **Access Mechanisms:**

  - *[Data Product Solution (PDP):]{.underline}* Pre‑prepared,
    analysis‑ready datasets available via the Enriched Clinical Primary
    data product in Starburst.

  - *[Golden Ticket Solution:]{.underline}* Process for accessing other
    study data (eg: raw SAS files) within the scope of an approved
    collection, initiated by users and granted within the 90:10 Open
    Access Model.

Why this matters:

- **Faster insights, better decisions:** More data ready‑to‑use, fewer
  hand‑offs, earlier cross‑study learning.

- **Lower friction, robust controls:** Streamlined access with privacy,
  security, and audit safeguards maintained.

## How It Works

90:10 Open Access Model combines two parts -- 'Top-Down Approval' and
'Rollout -- Comms & Solution'*:*

![A diagram of a process AI-generated content may be
incorrect.](media/image2.png){width="6.268055555555556in"
height="3.5027777777777778in"}

- **Top-Down Approval:** Delegate Data Owners work with the R&D Data
  Office to agree, up front, the specific Data Collections (studies/data
  sets), the permitted usages, and the user groups who can access them
  under a Data Use Terms (AoT). Approval is formally documented via
  the Open Access Collection (OAC) Agreement, and the Data Use Terms
  (AOT).

- **Rollout -- Comms & Solution**: Once approval is granted, you access
  the data by one of two solutions:

  - **Data Product Solution (eg: PDP)** - Proactively prepared
    SDTM-primary data exposed as database tables in Starburst. Access is
    granted in advance and consumed from analysis environments (no bulk
    copy).

  - **Golden Ticket Solution** - On-demand access to source files (e.g.,
    SAS files in entimICE, omics and imaging in IO Platform, Genestack,
    QuartzBio). Access is provisioned when requested, aligned to the
    approved collection.

## Primary Use vs Secondary Use Guidance

In June 2025 there was a Data Governance Board decision taken to expand
the data rights for drug development -- impacting how we can interpret
and apply Primary vs Secondary use of patient data.

This reinterpretation of Primary Use has brought about the following
clarifications:

- Reinforced AZ's position that primary use = study conduct + R&D
  activities supporting AZ drug development, across all data types and
  beyond TA

- Clarified that all data generated from a study + consented HBS sample
  reuse is study data available for this purpose

- Clarified that using AI to analyse data or applying an AI model to
  analyse patient study data is primary use

Below is a full **Position Statement** on Primary vs Secondary Use:

![](media/image3.png){width="5.865850831146107in"
height="3.322205818022747in"}

Adding to this, users should read below to understand the principles and
examples to help distinguish Primary from Secondary use:

**Primary Use:**

- [*Principle*]{.underline} = R&D activities supporting AZ drug
  development + Data use by AZ and third parties operating under AZ
  direction only

- *[Examples]{.underline}* = Include but are not limited to:

  - Conduct this trial and comply with regulatory obligations

  - Develop the drug/investigational product

  - Get permission to introduce and keep the drug on market

  - Monitor drug safety

  - Drug reimbursement activities

  - Related research activities necessary for drug development

  - Understand how the study drug and similar drugs work in the body

  - Better understand the studied disease and associated health problems

  - Develop diagnostics tests

  - Learn from past studies to plan new studies

  - Improve scientific analysis methods

  - Publish research results in scientific journals or use them for
    educational purposes

  - AI analytics ([excluding]{.underline} AI development and
    training)^3^

**Secondary Use**:

- *[Principle]{.underline}* = Future use scientific health-related
  research, within country limitations and subject to patient approval +
  data use by AZ or external partners under contract

- *[Examples]{.underline}* = Include but are not limited to:

  - AI development / training

  - External data sharing

Users are granted access to an *Open Access Collection* in line with the
agreement set out in the [Agreement of
Terms](https://azcollaboration.sharepoint.com/sites/VS2.Transformingdatasharingaccess2/_layouts/15/guestaccess.aspx?share=EZB-mbKKV8tGizhPWPzH5OQBzxEc9DU9d7ZZo3AKvYQKWg&e=8vkFoh)
-- approved by Delegate Data Owners. Under this agreement for the
BioPharma Closed Collection - users can only conduct analysis that
classifies as Primary Use as per the above definitions and explanation
-- Secondary Use is out of scope for this *Open Access Collection* -- as
in the Data Use Terms.

When accessing data, you are also responsible for handling data as
detailed in the mandatory training above (Global Standards, and SOP) --
this is also spelled out in the Data Use Terms. If any additional
questions remain or users are unsure on permitted use -- please refer to
the Support section below.

## Data Provisioning Checks

Before any access to the data is given, Data Provisioning Operations
(DPO) will make sure that all due diligence checks are performed
according to internal processes. This includes, but is not limited to:

- **Drug Product Rights:** AZ engages in mergers, acquisitions,
  strategic collaborations, and divestments of drug products. R&D Data
  Office actively manages records of drug product rights and reviews
  before data sharing

- **Cross Border Data Transfer Limitations:** Patient data is often
  considered a special class of data that deserves unique protections
  per country regulations. Where there are cross border data transfer
  limitations (such as HGR or US Data Security Rule) and/or intra-AZ
  group legal entity data sharing arrangements, the R&D Data Office
  reviews before data sharing.

- **Data Owner Approval:** Data Owners and their delegates within each
  TA govern "when" a clinical study is available for data sharing.
  Studies that have reached 6 months post final clinical database
  lock (CDL) are open by default, noting any business sensitivity. Data
  Owners are encouraged to define milestones where active/ongoing study
  or study parts auto approve, else manual approval

Studies that are included in an Open Access Collection will have already
gone through these checks -- and so users can be safe in the assumption
that studies available in the [90:10 Power BI Dashboard]{.mark} are
approved for use as in the *Data Use Terms*.

## Data use restrictions

### Permitted uses

> As per the agreement with the Data Owner (TALT Delegate), data in the
> ODS&AI AZ Closed Interventional Studies Data Collection may be used
> for purposes within the IMI wording agreed by the Data Governance
> Board (DGB) in June 2025. The purposes are:

- Understand how the study drug and similar drugs work in the body  

- Better understand the studied disease and associated health problems  

- Develop diagnostic tests for the disease  

- Learn from past studies to plan new studies  

- Improve scientific analysis methods  

- Publish research results in scientific journals or use them for
  educational purposes 

### Uses that are not permitted

> As per agreement with the Data Owner (TALT Delegate), data in the
> ODS&AI AZ Closed Interventional Studies Data Collection, must not be
> used to:

- Train or test AI models

- Any use outside the IMI wording agreed by the DGB in June 2025

Violation of these terms will result in the data access being revoked.

### Access environments

> Data provisioned through the 90:10 Open Access Model Data Collection
> method for ODS&AI AZ Closed Interventional Studies can be used within
> the Domino environment. This provisioning arrangement permits
> designated datasets to be accessed, processed, and analysed
> exclusively within the Domino infrastructure. All data usage shall
> remain within the boundaries of the Domino environment and must comply
> with applicable data governance policies and security protocols
> established for it.

## User responsibilities

### Responsibilities

All users of ODS&AI AZ Closed Interventional Studies Data Collection are
responsible for:

- Maintaining the confidentiality and data security,

- Adhere to the permitted uses,

- Report any suspected data breaches or unauthorised uses immediately
  (see [Incident management and breach
  notification](#_Incident_management_and)),

- Adhere to AZ additional policies and processes including, but not
  limited to:

  - Publication sign-off (PSO) process

  - List of relevant SOPs/Trainings

- Ensure that no direct or indirect identifiers, such as E-codes, are
  present in any materials that will go externally, e.g., publications
  and presentations,

- Take steps to mitigate the risk of re-identifying individuals,
  including accidentally through combining data sets,

> Immediately inform the project team of any medical or safety-related
> findings within a 24-hour timeframe.

**Choosing Your Path**:

When working with the *90:10 Open Access Model*, the path you\'ll take
depends on what type of data you need. Here\'s how to determine which
route is right for you:

- Use PDP when you need SDTM-primary data in native database form,
  accessed from analysis tools without data movement. This is suitable
  for approved analytics platform for a given Data Collection such as
  SCP, Domino, AI Bench, and entimICE where a Starburst endpoint is
  preferred.

- Use Golden Ticket when you need SAS files in an SAS environment or
  omics/imaging assets in IO Platform, Genestack, or QuartzBio. Note
  that access is granted on demand for an approved Data Collection.

If uncertain, consult the R&D Data Office to confirm the appropriate
solution for your study and environment.

## Close Down

Once your project is complete and results are achieved, it is the
project leader's responsibility to submit the [Project Completion
Form]{.mark} to remain compliant with the Global Standard. This ensures
that there is a documented record of how the data has been used, and the
results achieved. Data users can also use any of the existing mechanisms
for cataloguing and storing data products / analytical products that
might have been created (eg: catalogue in Collibra) to capture learning
and reusable assets to support future projects.
