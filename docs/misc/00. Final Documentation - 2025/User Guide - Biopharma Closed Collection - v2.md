# Awareness

## Purpose and Scope

The *Role-based Open Access Model* *(ROAM)* is a new R&D paradigm
rolling out from 2025 that gives approved users role‑based, default‑open
access to defined data collections -- reducing case‑by‑case approvals
while maintaining compliance. It is designed to accelerate impact while
managing risk. This guide sets out what's changing, how to access and
use data responsibly, and where to get support -- so you can move from
idea to outcome faster and compliantly.

By the end, you will know:

- What Open Access means for you and how it differs from
  project‑by‑project approvals

- The end‑to‑end journey for using Open Access Collections and the steps
  to follow

- What analysis intent is in scope (Primary Use) vs out of scope
  (Secondary Use)

- Your responsibilities for security, privacy, and publication

- How to plan, access, execute, and close-down a project within Open
  Access, and where to get help

We have structured this User Guide to align to the typical User Journey
(shown above) -- explaining how you discover, access, and use data under
the *ROAM* -- from initial notification to delivering a compliant
project, with support throughout. Below is an overview of the high-level
journey, with more details on each part in the sections below.

Steps you complete once (first-time setup):

- **Awareness:** *Upon inclusion in the 'Role-based Open Access Model',
  you will receive a contact email confirming your participation, along
  with links to the* *Role-based Open Access Model Knowledge Hub*
  *SharePoint page for communications materials.*

- **Compliance:** From the email, open the User Guide to review the
  model and mandatory training. After you have completed this training,
  the R&D Data Office will set up your access permissions in the
  relevant solutions (more below).

Steps you follow for each project:

- **Ideation:** use the [ROAM Power BI
  Dashboard](https://app.powerbi.com/groups/me/reports/61ad3ae5-e4c8-43dc-adcb-48a7d5aa7461/4d9e6c619e2d347100e8?ctid=af8e89a3-d9ac-422f-ad06-cc4eb4214314&experience=power-bi)
  to review available collections, each with specific scopes. Data
  Consumers should identify the collection that best aligns with their
  intended data use. Watch for new tooling as it becomes available.

- **Pre‑planning:** self‑check that your intended use aligns with the
  User Guide (Primary Use and any collection of constraints), contact
  the R&D Data Office if unsure.

- **Execution:** access data via the appropriate mechanism -- Data
  Product Solution (PDP) for pre‑prepared datasets, Entimice Access
  Process for on-demand use of original study assets in native platforms

- **Closedown:** Complete the short Project Completion Form to capture
  results and confirm outputs meet compliance requirements.

Support:

## What's New

The R&D Data Office is moving from individual, intent‑based approvals to
a role‑based open access model. The target operating principle is ROAM
-- where 90% of user needs are made open by default for qualified use,
while the remaining 10% (complex or edge cases) continue through
individual request-based models. Upfront, top‑down approvals and
proactive data preparation expand the accessible data estate and reduce
time‑to‑access, enabling broader, faster use.

Key changes we are introducing:

- **Role‑Based Access:** Permissions aligned to job role rather than
  project intent, with embedded privacy, security, and auditability.

- **Primary Use reinterpretation:** Data Governance Board decision to
  expand the interpretation of Primary Use (retiring Extended Primary
  Use -- EPU), unlocking more patient data for scientific analysis.

- ***Role-based Open Access Model*:** End‑to‑end operating process.
  Top‑down approval by Data Owners/Delegates coordinated rollout and
  stewardship by the R&D Data Office.

- **Access Mechanism:**

  - *[Data Product Solution (PDP):]{.underline}* Pre‑prepared,
    analysis‑ready datasets available via the Enriched Clinical Primary
    data product in Starburst

  - *[Entimice Access Process:]{.underline}* Another available 'Access
    Mechanism' for users to utilise raw SAS files in Entimice, in
    collaboration with GPPs

Why this matters:

- **Faster insights, better decisions:** More data ready‑to‑use, fewer
  hand‑offs, earlier cross‑study learning.

- **Lower friction, robust controls:** Streamlined access with privacy,
  security, and audit safeguards maintained.

## How It Works

The ROAM combines two parts -- 'Top-Down Approval' and 'Rollout -- Comms
& Solution'*:*

- **Top-Down Approval:** Delegate Data Owners work with the R&D Data
  Office to agree, up front, the specific Data Collections (studies/data
  sets), the permitted usages, and the user groups who can access them
  under a Data Use Terms (AoT). Approval is formally documented via
  the Open Access Collection (OAC) Agreement, and the Data Use Terms

- **Rollout -- Comms & Solution**: Once approval is granted, you must

  data through the available access mechanisms (see below for more
  details).

## Primary Use vs Secondary Use Guidance

In June 2025 there was a Data Governance Board decision taken to expand
the data rights for drug development -- impacting how we can interpret
and apply Primary vs Secondary use of patient data.

This reinterpretation of Primary Use has brought about the following
clarifications:

- Reinforced AZ's position that primary use = study conduct + R&D
  activities supporting AZ drug development, across all data types and
  beyond TA

- Clarified that all data generated from a study + consented HBS sample
  reuse is study data available for this purpose

- Clarified that using AI to analyse data or applying an AI model to
  analyse patient study data is primary use

Below is a full **Position Statement** on Primary vs Secondary Use:

Adding to this, users should read below to understand the principles and
examples to help distinguish Primary from Secondary use:

**Primary Use:**

- [*Principle*]{.underline} - R&D activities supporting AZ drug
  development + Data use by AZ and third parties operating under AZ
  direction only

- *[Examples]{.underline}* - Include but are not limited to:

  - Conduct this trial and comply with regulatory obligations

  - Develop the drug/investigational product

  - Get permission to introduce and keep the drug on market

  - Monitor drug safety

  - Drug reimbursement activities

  - Related research activities necessary for drug development

  - Understand how the study drug and similar drugs work in the body

  - Better understand the studied disease and associated health problems

  - Develop diagnostics tests

  - Learn from past studies to plan new studies

  - Improve scientific analysis methods

  - Publish research results in scientific journals or use them for
    educational purposes

  - AI analytics ([excluding]{.underline} AI development and training)

**Secondary Use**:

- *[Principle]{.underline}* - Future use scientific health-related
  research, within country limitations and subject to patient approval +
  data use by AZ or external partners under contract .

- *[Examples]{.underline}* - Include but are not limited to:

  - AI development/training

  - External data sharing

Users are granted access to an *Open Access Collection* in line with the
agreement set out in the Data Use Terms -- approved by Delegate Data
Owners. Under this agreement - users can only conduct analysis that
classifies as Primary Use as per the above definitions and explanation
-- Secondary Use is out of scope for this *Open Access Collection* -- as
in the Data Use Terms.

When accessing data, you are also responsible for handling data as
detailed in the [mandatory training](#mandatory-training). This is also
spelled out in the Data Use Terms. Please refer to the Support
section below if any additional questions remain, or users are unsure of
permitted use.

## Data Provisioning Checks

Before any access to the data is given, Data Provisioning Operations
(DPO) will make sure that all due diligence checks are performed
according to internal processes. This includes, but is not limited to:

- **Drug Product Rights:** AZ engages in mergers, acquisitions,
  strategic collaborations, and divestments of drug products. R&D Data
  Office actively manages records of drug product rights and reviews
  before data sharing

- **Cross Border Data Transfer Limitations:** Patient data often is
  considered a special class of data that deserves unique protections
  per country regulations. Where there are cross border data transfer
  limitations (such as HGR or US Data Security Rule) and/or intra-AZ
  group legal entity data sharing arrangements, the R&D Data Office
  reviews before data sharing.

- **Data Owner Approval:** Data Owners and their delegates within each
  TA govern "when" a clinical study is available for data sharing.
  Closed Studies, e.g. that have reached 6 months post final clinical
  database lock (CDL) are open by default, non-business sensitive. Data
  Owners are encouraged to define milestones where active/ongoing study
  or study parts auto approve, else manual approval

Studies that are included in an Open Access Collection will have already
gone through these checks -- and so users can be safe in the assumption
that studies available in the [ROAM Power BI
Dashboard](https://app.powerbi.com/groups/me/reports/61ad3ae5-e4c8-43dc-adcb-48a7d5aa7461/4d9e6c619e2d347100e8?ctid=af8e89a3-d9ac-422f-ad06-cc4eb4214314&experience=power-bi)
are approved for use as in the *Data Use Terms*.

## *Permitted uses*

As per the agreement with the Data Owner (TALT Delegate), data in the
Data Collection may be used for purposes within the IMI wording agreed
by the Data Governance Board (DGB) in June 2025. The purposes are: 

- *Conduct this trial and comply with regulatory obligations*

- *Develop the drug/investigational product*

- *Get permission to introduce and keep the drug on market*

- *Monitor drug safety*

- *Drug reimbursement activities*

- *Related research activities necessary for drug development*

- *Understand how the study drug and similar drugs work in the body*

- *Better understand the studied disease and associated health problems*

- *Develop diagnostics tests*

- *Learn from past studies to plan new studies*

- *Improve scientific analysis methods*

- *Publish research results in scientific journals or use them for
  educational purposes*

- *AI analytics ([excluding]{.underline} AI development and
  training)^3^*

## *Uses that are not permitted*

As per agreement with the Data Owner (TALT Delegate), data in the
Data Collection must not be used for: 

- *AI development/training*

- *External data sharing*

Violation of these terms will result in the data access being revoked

## *Access environments *

Data provisioned through the Role-based Open Access Model, and contained
in the Data Collection, can be used within existing R&D environments
that users currently have access to (eg: Domino, Entimice, etc) here we
are not introducing new analysis environments as part of this model.

All data usage shall remain within the boundaries of the chosen
analytics environment and must comply with applicable data governance
policies and security protocols established for it.

## *Prohibited activities *

Data Users are strictly prohibited from:  

- Attempting to identify individuals from the data, 

- Combining data with external datasets without approval, 

- Sharing data with unauthorised third parties and Users outside the
  approved group(s), 

- Reverse engineering or attempting to determine data sources, 

- Using data for competitive intelligence, 

- Moving the data out of the approved environment without approval

## *Data processing limitations*

All data processing activities must be conducted within geographic
locations and legal jurisdictions that comply with applicable
regulatory, contractual, and AstraZeneca organisational requirements.

## Execution

Request and use data through an approved access mechanism and follow the
analysis plan. Maintain good documentation, ensure reproducibility, and
adhere to governance, privacy, and collection of terms throughout.

- **Data Product Solution (PDP):** The Study Patient Data Product (SPDP)
  pipeline makes it easier for you to find and use patient data from
  closed studies. It takes data and standardises it into a common format
  (AZ SDTM standard), so you spend less time searching for and preparing
  data, and more time analysing it. All of this is available through
  Starburst, making the data accessible and ready to use across
  different studies and teams. Data provisioned through *ROAM* can be
  accessed in a number of PDP pipeline supported analysis environments.

  - *Practical Steps:*

    - *Connect to Starburst via your analysis environment (SCP, Domino,
      AI Bench, entimICE) following the technical connection guide.*

    - *No bulk data copy is needed - Query data on demand and datasets
      remain on the platform.*

    - *Access remains open until the study or user is removed from the
      collection. The R&D Data Office handles maintenance and
      revocation.*

  - *Solution Foundations:*

    - *entimICE as the data source for the primary SDTM (transition to
      LSAF over time).*

    - *Starburst hosts the data product as database tables for
      consumption.*

    - *Ranger as an access control mechanism policies map users to data
      products (planned replacement with Immuta in 2026)*

    - *Access and data consumption is enabled via SCP, Domino, AI Bench,
      and entimICE without copying datasets, query on demand.*

- **Entimice Access Process:** For users that require access to original
  study assets in native platforms, the Entimice Access Process is
  available -- whereby the R&D Data Office co-ordinates with the
  Entimice Support teams to provide approved users with access to study
  folders. Data Consumers can then analyse and use data through normal
  Entimice workflows, where permissions have been proactively granted.

  - ***Practical Steps:***

    - *Collections Manager - R&D Data Office ensures that eligible
      studies & users are listed in the Collections Manager as per
      Delegate Data Owner approval*

    - *Grant Folder Access - Collections manager submits request to
      create new user management group which contains approved users and
      which is mapped to study folders by support teams*

    - *Check Data Inclusion: Eligible & trained users check that they
      what data they have access to in existing collections*

    - *Continuous Use: Data access remains open until either the study /
      user is removed from the collection*

    - *Continuous Monitoring: Collection Managers monitor changes to
      collections based on changes to users and studies.*

    - *Access Permissions: User groups are updated to reflect changes to
      membership and included studies*

If uncertain, consult the R&D Data Office to confirm the appropriate
solution for your study and environment.

## Close Down

Once your project is complete and results are achieved, it is the
project leader's responsibility to submit the Project Completion Form to
remain compliant with the Global Standard. This ensures that there is a
documented record of how the data has been used, and the results
achieved. Data users can also use any of the existing mechanisms for
cataloguing and storing data products / analytical products that might
have been created (eg: catalogue in Collibra) to capture learning and
reusable assets to support future projects.

## Feedback

Effective feedback is essential for continuous improvement and
accountability. We encourage you to share thoughts openly. Both positive
experiences and areas for improvement are extremely valuable for us.

- What are we asking for?

> Share practical, actionable input on clarity of guidance, training,
> Terms of Use, connection steps (Patient Data Product), data scope and
> any friction point you encounter.

- How do you submit it?

> Use the single feedback form linked from this User Guide
> ([LINK](https://forms.office.com/e/4G34rUJuQV)) to capture
> issues, suggestions and improvements. For urgent access or compliance
> blockers, contact your Data Consumer Lead or the R&D Data Office at
> <DataOffice-Compliance@astrazeneca.com>. 

- When do you send it? 

> Submit feedback whenever it is convenient. The project team will
> triage the most significant ahead of the next phase. 

- What happens next?

> We will review and prioritise fixes and enhancements across people,
> processes, technology, and data. Updates to guidance, training, and
> collections will be communicated back, and changes will be tracked for
> transparency. 

- Keep it focused and compliant.

> Please avoid including patient-level/personal data or confidential
> content in free-text feedback. If you need to reference a dataset, use
> study identifiers.
